1. OVERVIEW
================================================================================

A 1v1 trading card game where players summon creatures, cast spells, manage
mana, and battle to reduce their opponent's life to zero.

================================================================================
2. SETUP & MULLIGAN
================================================================================

STARTING CONDITIONS:
- Each player starts with 30 life
- Each player draws 5 cards from their Main Deck
- Each player draws 2 cards from their Vault (land deck)
- Each player has a Leader card (displayed separately)
- Fields start empty, mana pools start empty

MULLIGAN PHASE:
Before the game begins, each player may choose to:
  - KEEP their hand, or
  - MULLIGAN (shuffle hand back into deck and draw 5 new cards)

Each player gets ONE mulligan decision. The game starts after both players
have decided.

================================================================================
3. TURN STRUCTURE
================================================================================

On your turn, you may (in any order):
  1. Play lands (max 1 per turn)
  2. Tap lands for mana
  3. Play creatures/spells/leader using mana
  4. Declare attacks with your creatures
  5. End your turn

AT END OF TURN:
  - Creatures with Vigilance untap

AT START OF YOUR NEXT TURN:
  - All your cards untap
  - Summoning sickness clears from your creatures
  - Your mana pool empties
  - Draw at least 1 card (draw up to minimum hand size if hand is smaller)

================================================================================
4. MANA SYSTEM
================================================================================

MANA TYPES:
  White (W), Blue (U), Black (B), Red (R), Green (G), Colorless

GENERATING MANA:

  Option 1 - TAP A LAND ON YOUR FIELD:
    Click a land you've played to tap it. Its mana is added to your pool.

  Option 2 - BURN A LAND FROM YOUR HAND:
    Discard a land card from your hand to add its mana to your pool.
    (The land goes to your discard pile)

SPENDING MANA:
  - Colored costs (White, Blue, etc.) must be paid with that exact color
  - Colorless costs can be paid with any color of mana
  - Unspent mana is lost at the start of your next turn

================================================================================
5. CARD TYPES
================================================================================

LANDS:
  - Free to play (no mana cost)
  - Maximum 1 land per turn
  - Enter the field untapped
  - Tap to produce mana of their color

CREATURES:
  - Pay the mana cost shown on the card
  - Enter the field with full health and untapped
  - Have SUMMONING SICKNESS - cannot attack the turn they're played
    (unless they have Haste)
  - Have Attack and Defense stats

SPELLS:
  - Pay the mana cost to cast
  - One-time effect, then goes to discard pile
  - Can only be played on your turn (during main phase)

INSTANTS:
  - Pay the mana cost to cast
  - One-time effect, then goes to discard pile
  - Can ONLY be played during the Combat Response Window
  - Used to buff creatures, deal damage, or control the battlefield

LEADERS:
  - Special creature card unique to your deck
  - Played like a creature (pay mana cost)
  - Usually has powerful stats and abilities
  - Displayed in a special area near your hand

================================================================================
6. COMBAT
================================================================================

STEP 1 - DECLARE ATTACKS:
  - Click "Combat" to enter combat mode
  - Select your untapped creatures (without summoning sickness) to attack
  - Choose targets: the opponent OR one of their creatures
  - Attacking creatures become tapped
  - Click "Confirm Attacks"

STEP 2 - RESPONSE WINDOW (Instants):
  After attacks are declared, both players get a chance to play Instants:

  - DEFENDER gets priority first
  - Play instants to buff your creatures, damage attackers, tap creatures, etc.
  - Click "Pass" to pass priority to opponent
  - Priority alternates until BOTH players pass consecutively
  - Then combat damage resolves

  INSTANT EFFECTS BY COLOR:
    White/Green - Buff your creatures (+Attack/+Defense)
    Red - Deal direct damage to creatures
    Blue - Tap creatures, bounce creatures to hand

STEP 3 - DAMAGE RESOLUTION:

  ATTACKS VS PLAYER:
    - Defender loses life equal to attacker's Attack

  ATTACKS VS CREATURE:
    - Both creatures deal damage to each other simultaneously
    - A creature dies when its health reaches 0 or below

  FIRST STRIKE:
    - Creatures with First Strike deal damage BEFORE normal creatures
    - If First Strike damage kills the opponent, they don't hit back

  DOUBLE STRIKE:
    - Deals damage in BOTH the First Strike phase AND normal phase
    - Effectively hits twice if it survives

  TRAMPLE:
    - Excess damage (beyond what kills the target) hits the defending player

TAUNT RESTRICTION:
  - If opponent has untapped creatures with TAUNT, you MUST attack them
  - Cannot attack the player directly while Taunt creatures are untapped
  - Cannot attack non-Taunt creatures while Taunt creatures are untapped

================================================================================
7. ABILITIES
================================================================================

HASTE
  Can attack the same turn it enters the battlefield.
  (Ignores summoning sickness)

VIGILANCE
  Untaps at end of your turn.
  (Can attack and still be ready to block on opponent's turn)

FLYING
  Can only be blocked by creatures with Flying or Reach.

REACH
  Can block creatures with Flying.
  (But cannot fly itself)

FIRST STRIKE
  Deals combat damage before creatures without First Strike.
  If it kills the opponent first, they don't deal damage back.

DOUBLE STRIKE
  Deals damage twice - once in First Strike phase, once in normal phase.

TRAMPLE
  Excess damage beyond the target creature's health goes through
  to the defending player.

TAUNT
  Enemy creatures MUST attack this creature if able.
  Protects your other creatures and your life total.

================================================================================
8. WINNING THE GAME
================================================================================

VICTORY:
  Reduce your opponent's life to 0 or below.
